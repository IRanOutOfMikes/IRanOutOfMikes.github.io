Is water wet or does it make things wet?

Water itself is not wet since wetness is defined as a sensation that results from being covered or saturated with a liquid. However, water can make things wet by coming into contact with them and causing them to become covered or saturated with the liquid. So, from the perspective of an object that is in contact with water, it would be accurate to say that water makes it wet. Ultimately, the answer to this question depends on how one chooses to define and interpret the concept of wetness.

How can I learn to hit a sick flip reset?

First, you'll need to score a double tap off the backboard and get all four wheels on the ball as it rebounds. Once all four wheels are touching the ball, jump and flip your car upside down while releasing your boost. As you approach the ball again, you'll need to hit jump to reset your flip, allowing you to flip into another aerial maneuver. Timing is crucial, so make sure to practice the move in free play or training mode until you can execute it consistently.

How does one "hit the dab"?

Start by raising one arm up to your face and tucking your head into the crook of your elbow. At the same time, extend your other arm out behind you. Hold the pose for a few seconds before relaxing and returning to a standing position. Repeat the movement with the opposite arm and continue alternating arms as desired.
